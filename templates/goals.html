Ultimate Goal

The Brain is an embodied intelligence that perceives, predicts, reasons, and learns. It can listen to sounds and watch scenes, predicting what comes next through its world model. It reasons across concepts via multi-hop spreading activation, understands causal relationships between events, composes new ideas from concept arithmetic, and knows its own strengths and weaknesses. Its curiosity-driven autonomy loop identifies weak areas and seeks to strengthen them. Language is grounded in experience — when it hears "thunder," its experiential memory of storms activates, not just a text match. It can imagine: chaining perception → prediction → causation → association into coherent thought. The next frontier: embodied action, persistent memory across sessions, and multi-agent collaboration.
